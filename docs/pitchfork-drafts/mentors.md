---

1. **Income and Wealth:** This dimension assesses individuals' economic well-being, including their income levels, wealth accumulation, and overall financial security.

2. **Education:** Education looks at access to quality education and lifelong learning opportunities. It considers factors like educational attainment, enrollment rates, and educational resources.

3. **Employment and Job Security:** This dimension focuses on the availability of decent employment opportunities, job stability, and work-life balance.

4. **Health and Wellness:** Health and wellness gauge physical and mental health, including factors like life expectancy, disease prevalence, healthcare access, and overall well-being.

5. **Personal Security:** Personal security addresses safety and protection from harm, both in physical and social contexts. It includes crime rates, social support networks, and factors that contribute to an individual's sense of safety.

6. **Social Inclusion:** Social inclusion considers factors that promote participation and engagement in society, such as social connections, belonging, and the absence of discrimination or exclusion.

7. **Environment:** This dimension evaluates the quality of the environment, including air and water quality, natural resources, and efforts toward sustainability and environmental protection.

8. **Leisure and Culture:** Leisure and culture encompass opportunities for recreation, cultural engagement, and participation in arts and community activities.
