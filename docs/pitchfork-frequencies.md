# Pitchfork Frequencies, Colors, and the Three Worlds

## Purpose

This document defines the relationship between:

* Pancakes life domains
* Pitchfork frequencies
* Color magic
* Commodities
* Real-world activities
* RPG resources
* Commerce-world assets

It introduces a unifying model that allows the same underlying phenomenon to appear differently in each layer of the ecosystem while remaining semantically connected.

The model explains how:

```text
A walk
becomes
a magical resource

A menstrual cycle
becomes
silver magic

A household chore
becomes
order magic

A contract
becomes
gold magic
```

without requiring separate systems for each interpretation.

---

# Core Principle

The ecosystem contains three simultaneous worlds:

## The Real World

The world of ordinary life.

Examples:

* walking
* sleep
* study
* caregiving
* menstruation
* budgeting
* cooking
* cleaning
* trade
* governance

This is where activities occur.

---

## The Magic World

The symbolic world.

Activities become:

* resources
* essences
* attunements
* spells
* rituals
* factions
* magical commodities

The player experiences this world through the RPG, ambient worlds, mentors, and symbolic projections.

---

## The Commerce World

The accounting world.

Activities become:

* events
* inventories
* commodities
* contracts
* covenants
* pools
* ledgers
* markets

This world is implemented through Pitchfork.

---

# Frequencies

A frequency is a recurring pattern of reality.

Examples:

* energy
* recovery
* growth
* cycles
* knowledge
* value
* governance

Frequencies exist simultaneously in all three worlds.

They are the bridge connecting:

```text
Real World
↔
Magic World
↔
Commerce World
```

---

# Colors

Colors are the symbolic representation of frequencies.

Each color represents:

* a life domain
* a magical school
* a category of commodities
* a civilization force

Colors are intentionally broader than elements.

They describe patterns of civilization.

---

# The Frequency Chain

Every event can be interpreted through the same structure:

Life Activity

# Corrupted Frequencies

Some commodities may produce distorted frequencies.

The first example is Oil.

---

## Oil

### Real World

Fossil fuels.

Stored ancient sunlight.

Industrial energy.

### Magic World

Corrupted Ember.

Deep Flame.

Ashfire.

### Commerce World

Energy markets.

Industrial dependence.

Resource extraction.

### Theme

Oil is powerful.

Oil is not evil.

However:

```text
High Power
+
High Dependency
=
Corruption Risk
```

Civilizations relying excessively on Oil Frequency may experience:

* ecological degradation
* imbalance
* fragility
* pollution

# The Three-World Projection Rule

Every meaningful thing should be representable in all three worlds.

Example:

| Real World      | Magic World   | Commerce World    |
| --------------- | ------------- | ----------------- |
| Walking         | Ember Moss    | Movement Event    |
| Sleep           | Lucid Dew     | Recovery Event    |
| Cleaning        | Order Salt    | Stewardship Event |
| Menstrual Cycle | Moonwater     | Cycle Event       |
| Coffee          | Roast Ember   | Coffee Commodity  |
| Contract        | Covenant Rune | Covenant Record   |
| Bitcoin         | Dragon Gold   | BTC Asset         |

This rule ensures that Pancakes, Pitchfork, and the RPG remain coherent interpretations of the same reality.

The player is never moving between disconnected systems.

They are viewing the same frequency through different lenses.
