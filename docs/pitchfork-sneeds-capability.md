# Pitchfork Sneeds Capability

## Purpose

This document defines a design requirement for the Pancakes and Pitchfork ecosystem:

> The ecosystem should be capable of adapting to unforeseen human needs, capacities, practices, and forms of value.

This requirement is illustrated through the hypothetical example of **sneeds**.

Sneeds are intentionally undefined.

They may represent:

* a newly discovered human need,
* a future technology-dependent requirement,
* a social capacity,
* a biological condition,
* a cultural practice,
* a civic obligation,
* or any future phenomenon not anticipated by the original system designers.

The purpose of sneeds is not to predict the future.

The purpose is to test whether the architecture can adapt to it.

---

# The Sneed Test

Imagine a future in which people discover that flourishing depends upon maintaining healthy sneeds.

Examples:

```text
Sneed Stability
Sneed Balance
Sneed Hygiene
Sneed Calibration
Sneed Literacy
```

People begin discussing sneeds.

Researchers publish findings.

Households begin managing sneeds.

Communities create institutions around sneeds.

Markets emerge around sneed-related goods and services.

The question is:

> Can the ecosystem support sneeds without requiring a complete redesign?

A successful architecture should answer:

> Yes.

---

# Architectural Requirement

The ecosystem should be designed around abstractions that can accommodate future domains.

It should avoid assumptions that:

* all important human needs are already known,
* current mentor domains are complete,
* current service categories are permanent,
* current flourishing models are final,
* current economic models are exhaustive.

Human societies continuously discover new needs, risks, opportunities, and forms of value.

The architecture should remain adaptable.

---

# Flourishing Framework Compatibility

The Human Flourishing Framework should be treated as extensible.

When sneeds appear, several possibilities exist.

## Case 1: Sneeds Support Existing Domains

Example:

```text
Sneed maintenance
→ increases safety
→ improves resilience
```

In this case, sneeds become another mechanism supporting existing flourishing capacities.

No change to the framework is required.

---

## Case 2: Sneeds Reveal Missing Structure

Example:

```text
Sneeds represent a form of flourishing
not captured by existing domains.
```

In this case:

* the framework is revised,
* new capacities are added,
* mentor mappings are updated,
* recipes evolve.

This is not a failure.

It is healthy adaptation.

The framework should remain open to revision.

---

# Recipe Compatibility

Recipes are intentionally domain-agnostic.

Example:

```yaml
recipe:
  purpose:
  participants:
  ingredients:
  process:
  settlement:
```

A Sneed Calibration recipe fits naturally within the existing structure.

Example:

```text
Recipe:
Monthly Sneed Review
```

or:

```text
Recipe:
Household Sneed Audit
```

No architectural changes are required.

---

# Grimoire Compatibility

Grimoires organize practical knowledge.

If sneeds become important, communities may create:

```text
Sneedcraft
Applied Sneedomics
Household Sneedkeeping
```

These become new grimoires.

The grimoire system requires no redesign.

Only new content.

---

# Mentor Compatibility

The mentor ecosystem should support both expansion and reinterpretation.

## Existing Mentor Absorption

An existing mentor may adopt responsibility for sneeds.

Examples:

```text
Foundry
→ economic sneeds

Stillwater
→ emotional sneeds

Hearthwright
→ maintenance sneeds
```

---

## New Mentor Emergence

If sneeds become sufficiently important, a new mentor may emerge.

Example:

```text
Mentor of Sneeds
```

The mentor ecosystem should support growth without destabilizing existing domains.

---

# Service Exchange Compatibility

The service exchange should support unforeseen services.

Example:

```text
Sneed Inspection
Sneed Maintenance
Sneed Repair
Sneed Consultation
```

The architecture should not require that service categories be known in advance.

New services should be introducible through configuration, governance, or community adoption.

---

# Household Compatibility

Households continuously manage forms of continuity.

Examples today:

```text
Food Continuity
Financial Continuity
Relationship Continuity
Maintenance Continuity
```

A future household might additionally manage:

```text
Sneed Continuity
```

Household systems should support the emergence of new stewardship responsibilities without requiring structural redesign.

---

# Pitchfork Event Compatibility

Pitchfork should be event-oriented rather than domain-specific.

Examples:

```text
sneed_review_completed

sneed_calibration_completed

sneed_maintenance_completed
```

The event architecture should permit new event types to emerge through governance and domain extension.

The system should not require hardcoded assumptions about all future forms of participation.

---

# Economic Compatibility

Future needs may generate new forms of economic activity.

The appearance of sneeds may produce:

* services,
* goods,
* professions,
* institutions,
* educational systems,
* cooperative support structures.

The economic architecture should be capable of representing these developments without privileging existing categories.

The key question remains:

> How does this activity contribute to flourishing?

Not:

> Was this activity anticipated by the original designers?

---

# Quality-of-Life Compatibility

Node Quality-of-Life systems should remain extensible.

If sneeds become important, indicators may evolve.

Examples:

```text
Sneed Stability

Sneed Access

Sneed Literacy
```

The QoL framework should support new indicators as societies learn more about flourishing.

---

# Governance Compatibility

Governance systems should support:

* creation of new domains,
* creation of new services,
* creation of new recipes,
* creation of new mentors,
* creation of new indicators,
* revision of flourishing models.

A flourishing ecosystem must remain capable of learning.

---

# The Principle Behind Sneeds

The Sneeds Capability is not actually about sneeds.

It is about humility.

The designers of any system should assume:

```text
There are important forms of value
we do not yet understand.
```

Future generations may discover:

* new needs,
* new capacities,
* new risks,
* new relationships,
* new forms of stewardship,
* new forms of flourishing.

The architecture should support discovery rather than resist it.

---

# Design Rule

A healthy ecosystem should require:

```text
new knowledge
```

rather than:

```text
new architecture
```

when unforeseen forms of flourishing emerge.

---

# Conclusion

The Pancakes and Pitchfork ecosystem should remain capable of supporting future forms of flourishing that are unknown today.

Recipes should adapt.

Grimoires should adapt.

Mentors should adapt.

Services should adapt.

Economic systems should adapt.

Governance should adapt.

The architecture should be sufficiently general that future societies can steward needs that have not yet been named.

Sneeds are a reminder that flourishing is larger than any framework, taxonomy, or generation.

The ecosystem should be built to learn.
