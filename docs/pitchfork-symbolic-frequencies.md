# Pitchfork Symbolic Frequencies

## Purpose

Symbolic frequencies provide the shared semantic foundation that allows Pancakes and Pitchfork to interpret ordinary life, symbolic play, civic participation, and economic activity as different views of the same underlying transformations.

This document is the canonical foundation for symbolic frequency concepts.

It defines:

* symbolic frequencies
* symbolic spectra
* frequency families
* provenance
* intrinsic and acquired meaning
* the relationship between transformations and projections

It does **not** define crafting operations, quests, recipes, contracts, or projection-specific behaviour. Those belong in their respective documents.

---

# Canonical Definition

A symbolic frequency is a recurring pattern of meaning that may appear across many different domains of life.

Examples include:

* movement
* learning
* stewardship
* exchange
* repair
* hospitality
* governance
* silver
* wetland
* spring

Symbolic frequencies are not:

* RPG statistics
* item tags
* arbitrary metadata
* enchantments
* gameplay power levels

Instead, they describe meaningful aspects of transformations that remain useful regardless of how those transformations are interpreted.

---

# Symbolic Spectra

Nothing is defined by a single symbolic frequency.

Every event, object, service, place, relationship, or artifact may be understood as having a symbolic spectrum.

A symbolic spectrum is the collection of symbolic frequencies associated with that thing.

For example:

```text
Community wetland cleanup

movement
stewardship
cooperation
wetland
spring
repair
place
```

A spectrum is a conceptual object.

It describes meaning.

This document intentionally does **not** require any particular mathematical representation.

Some implementations may use sparse vectors.

Others may use weighted graphs, symbolic relationships, derived values, or future representations.

The architecture defines meaning, not storage.

---

# Frequency Vectors

Frequency vectors are one possible implementation of symbolic spectra.

A vector represents independent symbolic dimensions.

Orthogonality means that one symbolic dimension does not erase another.

Walking through a wetland during a community cleanup may simultaneously contribute:

* movement
* wetland
* spring
* stewardship
* cooperation
* place

Each dimension remains independently meaningful.

This allows future capabilities to introduce new symbolic dimensions without changing existing semantics.

# Intrinsic And Acquired Frequencies

Some frequencies arise from identity.

Others arise from history.

## Intrinsic

Intrinsic frequencies describe what something is.

Examples:

* silver carries silver
* wood carries wood
* wheat carries wheat
* a wetland carries wetland

Intrinsic frequencies exist before any transformation occurs.

---

## Acquired

Acquired frequencies describe what has happened.

Examples:

* bread baked for a neighbour acquires hospitality
* silver recovered during creek restoration acquires stewardship
* a repaired bicycle acquires repair and continuity
* a community monument acquires cooperation

Meaning accumulates through transformation.

History matters.

---

# Provenance

Acquired frequencies are the symbolic expression of provenance.

Transformation leaves history.

History changes meaning.

Chemically identical objects may carry different symbolic spectra because they arrived through different transformations.

Examples include:

* reclaimed wood
* fair-trade coffee
* repaired tools
* handmade objects
* community-built infrastructure

Pitchfork preserves provenance because meaningful history should not disappear simply because an object's physical form changes.

---

# Place

Place contributes symbolic meaning without becoming a scoring system.

Places provide context.

Repeated transformations also change places.

A workshop accumulates craftsmanship.

A community garden accumulates stewardship.

A woodland trail accumulates memory.

Places therefore possess symbolic spectra just as objects and events do.

Different places are different.

They are not ranked.

---

# Capabilities

Capabilities expose coherent domains of life.

Examples:

* GIS
* barcode
* pollinator stewardship
* civic infrastructure
* inventory
* weather

Capabilities contribute symbolic dimensions by exposing relevant public facts.

They do not invent symbolic meaning.

Meaning arises from transformations interpreted within those domains.

---

# Projection

Symbolic frequencies are canonical.

Clients interpret them.

A single symbolic spectrum may appear differently depending on the projection.

Example:

```text
Transformation

walk through wetland

↓

Symbolic spectrum

movement
wetland
stewardship
spring

↓

Wellness

Walk completed.

↓

Commerce

Movement event settled.

↓

RPG

Ember Moss acquired.

↓

Ambient World

The woodland grows brighter.
```

No projection replaces another.

Each preserves different aspects of the same symbolic spectrum.

---

# Relationship To Crafting

Crafting transforms symbolic spectra.

Crafting may:

* refine
* amplify
* attenuate
* combine
* filter
* convert

Those operations are defined by symbolic crafting.

This document defines only the symbolic meaning that those operations manipulate.

---

# Design Principles

Meaning should survive transformation.

History should matter.

Provenance should remain visible unless intentionally transformed.

Different clients should share symbolic foundations.

No projection should become canonical.

Future symbolic domains should extend the architecture rather than replace it.

# Examples

A walk contributes movement.

A walk through Rouge wetlands during a stewardship event contributes movement, wetland, stewardship, place, and seasonal frequencies.

A tutoring session contributes learning, mentorship, exchange, and community frequencies.

A community meal contributes food, hospitality, cooperation, and neighbourhood frequencies.

Silver recovered during habitat restoration carries silver, stewardship, ecological, and place frequencies.

Different clients may interpret these spectra differently while preserving the same underlying transformation.
