# Pitchfork Symbolic Projections

## Purpose

Symbolic projections explain how a single underlying transformation can appear differently across multiple clients while preserving a shared symbolic foundation.

Transformations are canonical.

Symbolic spectra describe the meaning of those transformations.

Projections interpret symbolic spectra for particular purposes.

This document defines:

* symbolic projections
* projection boundaries
* projection families
* canonical relationships
* projection composition

It does **not** define symbolic frequencies, crafting operations, quests, application interfaces, or user interface design.

---

# Canonical Definition

A symbolic projection is a structured interpretation of a symbolic spectrum.

Projections do not create symbolic meaning.

They reveal selected aspects of symbolic meaning for a particular audience or purpose.

Different projections may emphasize different symbolic relationships while remaining faithful to the same underlying transformation.

---

# Canonical Structure

The symbolic architecture follows a consistent sequence.

```text id="l6a90w"
Transformation

↓

Symbolic Spectrum

↓

Projection

↓

Client Experience
```

Transformations change reality.

Symbolic spectra describe meaningful aspects of those transformations.

Projections interpret symbolic spectra.

Clients present those interpretations.

Every layer has a distinct responsibility.

---

# Why Projections Exist

Different people need different views of the same reality.

Examples include:

* a household
* a teacher
* a game player
* a cooperative
* a repair workshop
* a civic organization

Each may care about different aspects of the same transformation.

Rather than maintaining separate systems, Pitchfork preserves one symbolic foundation that supports many projections.

---

# Canonical Principle

No projection is canonical.

The transformation is canonical.

Different projections should preserve meaningful continuity rather than competing to become the "true" representation.

---

# Projection Families

Different clients naturally produce different kinds of projections.

---

## Human

Human projections describe ordinary life.

Examples include:

* household review completed
* meal prepared
* bicycle repaired
* tutoring session completed
* stewardship activity recorded

These emphasize understandable human experience.

---

## Commerce

Commerce projections describe accounting and exchange.

Examples include:

* inventory updated
* covenant settled
* service completed
* ownership transferred
* credit earned

These emphasize economic relationships.

---

## Symbolic

Symbolic projections interpret symbolic spectra through stories, mentors, rituals, schools, artifacts, and symbolic resources.

Examples include:

* Ember Moss
* Traveller's Charm
* purification
* lunar silver
* sanctuary stability

These emphasize symbolic teaching rather than accounting.

---

## Ambient

Ambient projections communicate symbolic state through environments.

Examples include:

* forests become brighter
* workshops become warmer
* birds return
* rivers become calmer
* gardens flourish

Ambient projections communicate through atmosphere rather than explicit mechanics.

---

## Educational

Educational projections emphasize learning.

Examples include:

* new competency demonstrated
* mentoring milestone
* stewardship experience
* scientific observation
* historical understanding

These emphasize growth rather than rewards.

---

## Community

Community projections describe collective participation.

Examples include:

* neighbourhood stewardship
* festival preparation
* shared repair history
* commons activity
* community resilience

These emphasize relationships among people.

---

Future projection families may emerge without changing the symbolic foundation.

---

# Projection Responsibility

Each projection chooses what to emphasize.

It should not redefine symbolic meaning.

For example:

```text id="7kskxp"

care

↓

Commerce

Repair service completed.

↓

Educational

Maintenance skill practiced.

↓

RPG

Forge strengthened.
Iron restored.

↓

Ambient

Workshop glows warmly.
```

Every projection preserves continuity.

None replaces another.

---

# Projection Fidelity

Different projections preserve different aspects of symbolic spectra.

A projection may:

* simplify
* summarize
* symbolize
* aggregate
* visualize
* narrate

It should not invent contradictory symbolic meaning.

Projection is interpretation rather than fabrication.

---

# Projection Composition

Multiple projections may exist simultaneously.

A household dashboard may display:

* inventory
* wellness
* stewardship
* symbolic activity

An RPG client may display:

* relics
* essences
* mentors
* sanctuaries

A civic dashboard may display:

* volunteer participation
* habitat restoration
* infrastructure relationships

All describe the same underlying transformations.

---

# Projection Independence

Applications remain independent.

A new client should not require changes to symbolic frequencies or symbolic crafting.

Instead:

```text id="llrjlwm"
Transformation

↓

Symbolic Spectrum

↓

New Projection
```

This allows future applications to emerge without redesigning the symbolic foundation.

---

# Projection And Privacy

Projections should respect visibility.

Different viewers may receive different projections of the same symbolic spectrum.

For example:

A household may see detailed activity history.

A community dashboard may show only aggregate stewardship.

An RPG client may show symbolic resources.

A public display may show only environmental ambience.

Projection should preserve privacy while remaining meaningful.

---

# Projection And Place

Places also participate in projection.

The same woodland may appear as:

Human:

Community woodland.

Commerce:

Managed conservation area.

Educational:

Outdoor classroom.

Ambient:

Living sanctuary.

RPG:

The Emerald Grove.

The place remains the same.

Only the interpretation changes.

---

# Projection And Provenance

Projections should preserve provenance whenever practical.

A handcrafted object and a mass-produced object may appear different because their symbolic spectra differ.

The projection should reveal meaningful history rather than replacing it with arbitrary rarity or power.

---

# Design Principles

Transformations are canonical.

Symbolic spectra are shared.

Projections interpret rather than redefine.

Different clients should coexist.

Meaning should remain consistent across projections.

Applications should remain independent.

Privacy should be preserved.

New projections should compose naturally with existing symbolic foundations.
