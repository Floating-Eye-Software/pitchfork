
# Magic System in Pitchfork

## Overview

The magic system in **Pitchfork** is an intricate blend of traditional color magic and contract-based magic, shaped by the interplay between two worlds: one steeped in mystical energies and the other driven by commerce and contracts. Magic is fueled by **Essence Tokens** and manipulated through **colored tokens**, while **contracts** enable strategic interaction between players in the magical and economic realms.

## Color Magic

### Skill Categories

Magic is categorized into six distinct **skill categories**, each represented by a different color. These categories represent different magical abilities that players can master over time. Each color corresponds to a unique branch of magic, and tokens of these colors are imbued with various magical powers.

- **Red (Fire Magic)**: Represents offensive, aggressive magic, dealing with fire, destruction, and combat.
- **Orange (Transformation Magic)**: Focuses on changing forms, whether it's altering matter or shapeshifting.
- **Yellow (Light Magic)**: Deals with healing, protection, and illumination. Yellow magic is often defensive or supportive.
- **Green (Nature Magic)**: Involves growth, healing, and communion with natural forces such as plants, animals, and the earth.
- **Blue (Water Magic)**: Represents fluidity and adaptability, influencing water, weather, and time manipulation.
- **Purple (Illusion Magic)**: Focuses on illusions, deception, mind control, and bending perceptions.

### Colored Tokens

Colored tokens correspond to the six skill categories. These tokens come in **common**, **rare**, and **legendary** forms, based on the difficulty of the magical task completed to earn them. The potency of spells depends on the rarity of tokens used and the combination of different colors.

### Blankening and Essence Tokens

In order to cast powerful spells or create potent contracts, players must often "blanken" their colored tokens. **Blankening** releases the magic stored in colored tokens, converting them into **Essence Tokens**—a gray, neutral form of magical energy that can be used universally.

Essence Tokens can then be:
- Used as fuel for spells or to perform certain magical actions.
- Imbued back into tokens or contracts.
- Traded for commodities or in-game resources.

## Contract Magic

### Introduction to Contracts

Contracts are magical documents that bind players to certain terms, creating a link between the **Magical Realm** and the **Parallel World of Commerce**. Contracts function as challenges or tasks that one player (the questgiver) sets for another player (the quester), often with rewards offered in the form of tokens or Essence Tokens.

Contracts also introduce an economic layer to the magic system, allowing players to engage in strategic, collaborative, and competitive interactions within the magical community.

### Types of Contracts

Contracts can vary in their complexity and rewards, providing flexibility to players:
- **Basic Contracts**: Quests involving simple tasks such as collecting resources or casting a specific spell.
- **Advanced Contracts**: More challenging quests requiring the completion of difficult magical tasks or the gathering of rare tokens.
- **Commodity Contracts**: Special contracts that involve the **conversion of commodities** into magical energy, introducing a unique dynamic where real-world economic factors influence the magic system.

### The Binding Nature of Contracts

Once a contract is agreed upon, it becomes **binding**—failure to complete a contract results in penalties, while successful completion results in rewards. Players can offer colored tokens, Essence Tokens, or fractional ownership in commodities as rewards.

## Commodities and Magic

### Commodities as Magical Resources

The parallel world of commerce introduces **commodities** such as oil, coffee, wheat, silver, and gas into the magic system. These commodities possess their own form of magical essence, and players can convert them into tokens with specific powers. The essence that each commodity holds is influenced by the nature of that commodity.

- **Oil and Gas**: Embues tokens with dark, powerful, and often destructive magic. This might manifest as curses or potent offensive spells.
- **Coffee**: Infuses tokens with a stimulant-like energy, granting speed and focus to the caster. Spells could enhance mental clarity or boost physical reflexes.
- **Wheat**: Represents growth, harvest, and nourishment, embuing tokens with nature magic, healing powers, and the ability to foster regeneration.
- **Silver**: Conveys purity and protective magic, often used in crafting wards, shields, and spells of purification.

The **market value** of these commodities fluctuates over time, which in turn influences their **in-game magical potency**. If the price of silver rises in the real world, for example, the magic derived from silver will become stronger within the game.

### Conversion Process

Players can convert Essence Tokens into fractional shares of virtual commodities, and through a process called **conversion**, they can transform these commodities into newly colored tokens. For instance:
- A player might trade Essence Tokens for shares in wheat, then use the wheat's essence to create **Green Tokens** (Nature Magic).
- Players can also reverse the process, converting colored tokens into commodities, adding a unique economic layer to gameplay.

## Scores and Progression

To track players’ mastery over the magical system and their success within the economy, **Pitchfork** uses a system of scores that reflect different aspects of gameplay:

- **Skill Enhancement Score**: Tracks the player’s progress across the six magical categories and their mastery of spells.
- **Contract Completion Score**: Measures the value of tokens earned through completed contracts, emphasizing a player's ability to fulfill quests.
- **Commodity Conversion Score**: Reflects the success of converting commodities into magical tokens and how well a player manipulates real-world market conditions.
- **Market Success Score**: Tracks achievements in in-game market transactions, showing proficiency in economic strategies.
- **Quester Score**: Measures tokens earned by completing other players' contracts.
- **Questgiver Score**: Tracks tokens given to other players via contracts, showcasing leadership and strategic planning.

---

## Conclusion

The **magic system in Pitchfork** combines traditional color-based magic with the economic power of contracts and commodities. Players engage in a fluid system where **colored tokens**, **Essence Tokens**, and **contract magic** interact, offering a balance of strategic, magical, and economic gameplay. With real-world commodities influencing magical power, players are challenged to navigate both mystical and economic forces, shaping the world around them through contracts, quests, and spells.
