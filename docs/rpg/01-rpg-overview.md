# Pitchfork RPG Overview

## Introduction

The Pitchfork RPG is a symbolic role-playing game built on top of the Pitchfork accounting engine and the Pancakes ecosystem.

At first glance it appears to be a fantasy role-playing game about magic, crafting, exploration, contracts, and the growth of a magical civilization. Beneath that experience lies a broader purpose: the RPG serves as an educational platform, an economic research sandbox, and a practical client for the Pancakes ecosystem.

Rather than separating games from everyday life, the Pitchfork RPG intentionally connects them. Real-world activity can become meaningful participation within a shared symbolic world, while discoveries made inside that symbolic world can inform the future development of the Commonwealth itself.

The result is simultaneously:

* a browser RPG,
* a life-powered gathering and crafting game,
* a symbolic economic simulation,
* a pedagogical experience,
* a research platform,
* a testbed for Commonwealth institutions,
* and a client built on the Pitchfork platform.

---

# Relationship to Pitchfork

Pitchfork provides the underlying accounting, contracts, resources, identity, and event model.

The RPG is one possible interpretation of those underlying systems.

Rather than presenting users with ledgers, contracts, inventories, and projections directly, the RPG expresses them as magical concepts:

* magical resources,
* rituals,
* covenants,
* relics,
* alchemy,
* magical contracts,
* guilds,
* sanctuaries,
* and magical economies.

The game does not replace Pitchfork.

It demonstrates how a symbolic world can emerge from the same accounting primitives that may later support many other applications.

---

# Relationship to Pancakes

The RPG is also a Pancakes client.

Pancakes records meaningful human participation across many domains of life, such as learning, stewardship, health, caregiving, creativity, household management, and community participation.

Within the RPG, these activities become symbolic experiences.

Examples include:

* walking becoming the gathering of magical resources,
* studying becoming the recovery of forgotten runes,
* household care becoming sanctuary maintenance,
* cooking becoming alchemy,
* journaling becoming memory magic,
* caregiving becoming hearth magic.

The player is not rewarded simply for completing tasks.

Instead, lived experience is interpreted through the symbolic language of the magical world.

# More Than A Game

The RPG has several complementary purposes.

## A Fantasy RPG

Players explore a magical civilization.

They learn spells.

Gather resources.

Craft artifacts.

Join guilds.

Complete quests.

Develop magical specialties.

Build sanctuaries.

Participate in a living world.

---

## A Pedagogical Experience

The game teaches the concepts underlying the Pancakes Commonwealth through symbolic discovery rather than formal instruction.

Players are introduced to increasingly sophisticated ideas through magical concepts including:

* color magic,
* alchemy,
* blankening,
* contracts,
* institutions,
* governance,
* stewardship,
* and civilization building.

The player learns these concepts as discoveries made by novices rather than as lectures delivered by the designers.

---

## An Economic Sandbox

The magical economy serves as a safe environment for experimenting with economic systems before they affect real communities.

Players can explore:

* resource systems,
* exchange,
* contracts,
* cooperation,
* governance,
* institutional design,
* symbolic currencies,
* incentives,
* and stewardship.

Ideas can be explored safely inside the game before informing future Commonwealth development.

---

## A Research Platform

The long-term goal is not merely to create players who understand the existing system.

The goal is to create players capable of improving it.

The RPG encourages experimentation with:

* new magical schools,
* new rituals,
* new resources,
* new institutions,
* new governance models,
* new economic mechanisms,
* and new forms of cooperation.

Players become contributors to an evolving symbolic civilization rather than consumers of a fixed game.

---

# The Core Design Philosophy

Most games reward players for defeating enemies or accumulating wealth.

The Pitchfork RPG instead focuses on stewardship.

Players cultivate:

* themselves,
* their households,
* their guilds,
* their communities,
* and eventually entire civilizations.

Power is not measured solely by combat ability.

Instead it emerges from knowledge, craftsmanship, cooperation, trust, and responsible stewardship.

The game celebrates creation more than conquest.

## The Commerce World

A symbolic representation of institutions, contracts, ownership, governance, exchange, and cooperation.

Initially this exists entirely within the fictional setting.

---

# The Novice's Journey

Players begin as novices within a magical academy.

Their mentors teach the established traditions of magic.

As their understanding grows, the novices begin making discoveries beyond the academy's existing knowledge.

Each discovery introduces a deeper conceptual layer of the world.

The player gradually progresses from learning individual spells to understanding magical economies, institutions, stewardship, and the foundations of civilization itself.

The central narrative is therefore not the story of a chosen hero.

It is the story of an expanding understanding.

---

# One World, Many Clients

The browser RPG is intended to be the first expression of the Pitchfork world rather than the only one.

Future clients may include:

* browser interfaces,
* mobile applications,
* ambient simulations,
* multiplayer worlds,
* educational tools,
* household management applications,
* health and wellness experiences,
* and additional game clients.

Each client interprets the same underlying Pitchfork platform through its own symbolic language while sharing common identity, resources, accounting, and history.

---

# Long-Term Vision

The long-term vision is a persistent symbolic civilization whose economy is powered by human participation rather than abstract game mechanics alone.

Players learn through exploration.

Experiment through play.

Contribute through stewardship.

Discover new institutions.

And ultimately help shape the future evolution of both the fictional world and the real-world Commonwealth it was designed to explore.

The Pitchfork RPG is therefore not simply a fantasy game.

It is a shared symbolic language for exploring how people create value, cooperate, govern institutions, and build civilizations together.
