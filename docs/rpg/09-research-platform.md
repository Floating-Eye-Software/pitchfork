# The Research Platform

## Introduction

The Pitchfork RPG is designed to become more than a game.

It is intended to become a collaborative research platform where players, designers, educators, economists, software developers, artists, and community organizers work together to explore better ways of building civilizations.

The game world provides a shared symbolic language.

The community provides imagination.

Together they create an ongoing process of experimentation, observation, and improvement.

The goal is not to discover a single perfect design.

The goal is to cultivate a civilization capable of learning.

---

# Players Become Researchers

Traditional games ask players to master existing systems.

The Pitchfork RPG asks players to extend them.

As players mature, they transition through several roles.

Initially they are:

* novices,
* explorers,
* apprentices,
* and students.

Later they become:

* researchers,
* builders,
* teachers,
* guild leaders,
* institution designers,
* and stewards.

The ultimate progression is intellectual rather than numerical.

---

# The Academy Never Ends

Graduation from the Academy is not the conclusion of learning.

It marks the beginning of independent research.

Players continue asking questions such as:

* What have we overlooked?
* Could this ritual work differently?
* Can we invent a new magical discipline?
* How should this institution evolve?
* What happens if these two systems interact?

The Academy prepares students to continue discovering long after formal instruction ends.

---

# Open Discovery

The symbolic world should never be considered complete.

Players are encouraged to discover:

* new magical schools,
* symbolic relationships,
* resources,
* creatures,
* recipes,
* rituals,
* institutions,
* governance systems,
* educational practices,
* and entirely new forms of gameplay.

Discovery is treated as an ordinary part of participation.

The world grows because its players grow.

---

# Collaborative Knowledge

Knowledge belongs to the community.

Players preserve discoveries through:

* libraries,
* grimoires,
* research journals,
* guild archives,
* teaching,
* mentorship,
* and collaborative experimentation.

Every generation inherits the work of those who came before while extending it further.

Civilization remembers through shared knowledge.

---

# Community Laboratories

Guilds naturally become research communities.

Different groups may investigate:

* magical theory,
* alchemy,
* ecology,
* contracts,
* governance,
* symbolic materials,
* economics,
* education,
* architecture,
* or entirely new disciplines.

Different communities may reach different conclusions.

This diversity strengthens the world rather than fragmenting it.

---

# Modding As Research

The Pitchfork RPG should be designed to encourage extension.

Players and developers should eventually be able to create:

* quests,
* magical schools,
* rituals,
* recipes,
* resources,
* creatures,
* sanctuaries,
* institutions,
* governance models,
* educational content,
* and entirely new game systems.

Mods are not merely additional content.

They are research proposals expressed through gameplay.

---

# Experiments

The platform should make experimentation easy.

Communities should be able to ask questions such as:

* Does this institution encourage cooperation?
* Does this magical resource create interesting gameplay?
* Can this governance model scale?
* What incentives produce healthier communities?
* Does this ritual improve learning?
* What happens if this assumption changes?

Experiments should be repeatable.

Results should be discussable.

Unexpected outcomes should be welcomed.

---

# Observation

Every experiment produces observations.

Researchers should examine:

* player behavior,
* institutional stability,
* cooperation,
* resource circulation,
* governance outcomes,
* educational effectiveness,
* and long-term sustainability.

Observation is more valuable than defending preconceived ideas.

The world should continually surprise its designers.

---

# Multiple Civilizations

There should never be only one correct civilization.

Different communities may organize themselves differently.

One guild may emphasize scholarship.

Another may prioritize ecological stewardship.

Another may explore decentralized governance.

Another may develop entirely new magical traditions.

The platform exists to compare ideas rather than eliminate alternatives.

---

# The Role of Technology

Technology supports research.

It does not replace it.

The underlying Pitchfork platform provides tools for:

* accounting,
* contracts,
* identities,
* resources,
* recipes,
* projections,
* and event histories.

The symbolic world allows people to reason about those systems collaboratively.

Technology provides the infrastructure.

People provide imagination, judgment, and stewardship.

---

# Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence may eventually assist research.

Possible roles include:

* mentors,
* librarians,
* archivists,
* tutors,
* world simulation,
* content generation,
* balancing,
* and design assistance.

AI should amplify human creativity rather than replace human decision-making.

The platform remains fundamentally human-centered.

---

# From Player To Contributor

One of the central goals of the platform is to blur the distinction between players and developers.

As understanding grows, players naturally begin contributing:

* ideas,
* documentation,
* artwork,
* software,
* educational material,
* governance proposals,
* research,
* and community leadership.

Participation gradually shifts from consumption toward co-creation.

---

# Connection To The Commonwealth

The symbolic civilization exists alongside the Pancakes Commonwealth.

Ideas developed inside the RPG may eventually influence real-world systems.

Likewise, practical experience from real communities may inspire new magical institutions within the game.

Neither world dominates the other.

Instead they engage in an ongoing conversation.

The symbolic world provides imagination.

The real world provides experience.

Together they evolve.

---

# Open Questions

The platform intentionally preserves unanswered questions.

Examples include:

* Which institutions deserve long-term study?
* Which governance structures remain unexplored?
* Which symbolic systems best support learning?
* Which forms of cooperation are missing?
* What new magical disciplines have yet to be discovered?

These unanswered questions invite continued participation.

The platform remains permanently open to discovery.

---

# Success

The research platform succeeds when players begin proposing ideas that surprise its original designers.

A successful player eventually says:

> "I think there is a better way."

And then possesses the symbolic language, technical tools, community support, and experimental environment needed to explore that idea.

At that point the RPG has fulfilled its deepest purpose.

It has transformed players into researchers, researchers into builders, and builders into stewards of an evolving civilization.

The Pitchfork RPG is therefore not simply software.

It is a living research community built around a shared symbolic world—one in which every new generation inherits centuries of accumulated knowledge, contributes discoveries of its own, and leaves the civilization more capable than it found it.
