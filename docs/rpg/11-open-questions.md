# Open Questions

## Introduction

The Pitchfork RPG is intentionally unfinished.

Neither the designers nor the Academy claim to possess complete knowledge of the world.

Many questions remain unanswered.

Some are technical.

Some are philosophical.

Some concern gameplay.

Others concern civilization itself.

These open questions are not defects.

They are invitations to participate.

Every unanswered question represents an opportunity for future players, researchers, designers, and communities to contribute to the evolution of the world.

---

# The Philosophy of Open Questions

The purpose of this document is not to maintain a backlog of software tasks.

Software implementation belongs elsewhere.

Instead, this document records questions that shape the future direction of the symbolic world.

Many of these questions may never have a single correct answer.

Different communities may legitimately arrive at different conclusions.

The platform is designed to support exploration rather than enforce uniformity.

# The Long View

The Pitchfork RPG is not expected to reach a final, completed state.

Like the Academy itself, it should remain capable of learning.

Each generation inherits the discoveries of those who came before.

Each generation asks new questions.

Each generation leaves behind new knowledge.

The platform succeeds when future contributors are able to surprise its original designers.

# An Invitation

Every player begins as a novice.

Some eventually become scholars.

Some become builders.

Some become teachers.

Some become stewards.

Some become researchers.

The questions in this document exist because the future of the world has not yet been written.

If you encounter a question that inspires you, you have found an opportunity to contribute.

The Academy does not seek followers who merely preserve tradition.

It seeks people willing to explore unknown territory with curiosity, humility, and a commitment to leaving the world wiser than they found it.
