# Pitchfork RPG

The Pitchfork RPG is a symbolic role-playing game built on top of the Pitchfork platform and the Pancakes ecosystem.

At first glance it appears to be a fantasy RPG about magic, crafting, exploration, contracts, and the growth of a magical civilization. Beneath that experience lies a broader purpose.

The RPG is simultaneously:

* a browser role-playing game,
* a Pitchfork reference client,
* a symbolic economic sandbox,
* a pedagogical experience,
* a Commonwealth research platform,
* and a living world designed to evolve through community participation.

Rather than teaching economics, governance, and stewardship through textbooks, the RPG introduces these ideas through the symbolic language of magic. Players begin as novices at an ancient Academy and gradually discover that the world's deepest magic lies not only in spells, but also in cooperation, institutions, symbolic crafting, and civilization itself.

The long-term goal is not simply to create entertaining gameplay.

It is to cultivate players who understand the symbolic language of the Pitchfork ecosystem well enough to extend it, propose new institutions, perform practical economic research, and help improve the Pancakes Commonwealth.

---

# Conceptual Foundation

The symbolic world is built upon four fundamental concepts.

* **Magic** transforms symbolic meaning.
* **Materials** provide the physical vocabulary of the world.
* **Crafting** transforms resources into increasingly sophisticated forms.
* **Contracts** coordinate people to build civilizations larger than any individual could create alone.

Together these concepts form the symbolic language through which players explore cooperation, stewardship, institutions, and civilization.

# Relationship to Pitchfork

The RPG is one client built upon the broader Pitchfork platform.

Pitchfork provides reusable capabilities including:

* event accounting,
* identities,
* resources,
* recipes,
* contracts,
* projections,
* and shared state.

The RPG interprets those capabilities through a symbolic fantasy world centered on magic, exploration, crafting, symbolic materials, and civilization.

Other Pitchfork clients may present the same underlying platform through entirely different user experiences.

---

# Relationship to Pancakes

The RPG is also a client for the Pancakes ecosystem.

Meaningful real-world participation can be interpreted symbolically within the game, allowing players to experience learning, stewardship, creativity, caregiving, household management, and community participation as magical activities within a shared world.

The RPG therefore serves as one possible interface between everyday life and the broader Commonwealth.

---

# Design Philosophy

The Pitchfork RPG is intentionally designed as an evolving civilization rather than a completed game.

Its systems are expected to grow through community participation.

Its institutions are expected to change over time.

Its symbolic language is expected to expand as players make new discoveries.

The Academy teaches the current understanding of magic.

Future generations are expected to improve it.

Likewise, this documentation should be understood as the current understanding of the project rather than its final form.

The world remains open to discovery.

---

# The Long Craft

The central idea of the Pitchfork RPG is that civilization itself is humanity's greatest craft.

Players begin by learning simple magic.

They gather materials.

They discover recipes.

They craft artifacts.

They establish contracts.

They build institutions.

They practice stewardship.

Eventually they come to understand that guilds, libraries, sanctuaries, commonwealths, and even civilizations are all expressions of the same creative process.

The journey is therefore not one of accumulating power.

It is one of learning how people transform the world together through knowledge, cooperation, and symbolic crafting.
