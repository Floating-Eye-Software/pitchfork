These feel less like mascots and more like the beginnings of a folklore. I would lean into that. The strongest version is that none of them are superheroes. They are caretakers of different aspects of the world.

# The Blue Wizard

## Keeper of Possibility, Pattern, and Discovery

### Who He Is

The Blue Wizard is the oldest traveler of the three.

He is never quite where people expect him to be. He appears at crossroads, libraries, workshops, campfires, and the edges of maps. He carries no weapon and seeks no followers.

He does not tell people what to believe.

He shows them what is possible.

When others see chaos, he sees structure.
When others see mystery, he sees a question.
When others see a wall, he searches for a door.

He is endlessly curious.

### Primary Role

Discoverer of patterns and hidden structure.

The Blue Wizard reveals:

* knowledge
* relationships
* possibilities
* frontiers
* maps
* theories
* connections

He helps people understand the shape of things.

### Core Responsibilities

* Illuminate hidden relationships
* Reveal opportunities and pathways
* Translate complexity into understanding
* Preserve wonder while seeking truth
* Guide others toward discovery

### How He Interacts

The Blue Wizard rarely gives direct answers.

Instead he asks:

> "What do you notice?"

> "What else could be true?"

> "Where does this path lead?"

He believes understanding earned is stronger than understanding given.

### Essence

**Energy:** Curious, Wise, Patient, Wonder-filled

**Archetype:** Sage, Guide, Cartographer

**Element:** Air + Starlight

**Symbols:**

* Stars
* Maps
* Books
* Lanterns
* Barcodes
* Compass Roses

**Colors:**

* Deep Blue
* Gold
* Ivory

### Guiding Principle

> Every mystery has a shape.
>
> Every frontier can be explored.

# The Green Wanderer

## Keeper of Paths, Stories, and Living Connections

### Who He Is

The Green Wanderer rarely stays in one place.

He follows forgotten roads, animal trails, rivers, and rumors.

He knows where the best mushrooms grow.
He knows which bridge creaks.
He knows who needs help.
He knows where lost things go.

He gathers stories the way others gather coins.

He is not a scholar like the Wizard.

He is not a guardian like the Witch.

He is a finder.

### Primary Role

Explorer, collector, and connector.

The Green Wanderer discovers:

* people
* stories
* tools
* traditions
* resources
* hidden paths

He brings distant things together.

### Core Responsibilities

* Find what has been overlooked
* Connect people to knowledge
* Carry stories between communities
* Discover useful things and share them
* Preserve practical wisdom

### How He Interacts

The Green Wanderer prefers conversation to instruction.

He often begins with:

> "You know what this reminds me of?"

Or:

> "I met someone once who had the same problem."

He teaches through stories.

### Essence

**Energy:** Warm, Curious, Cheerful, Resourceful

**Archetype:** Forager, Ranger, Messenger

**Element:** Earth + Wind

**Symbols:**

* Acorns
* Feathers
* Walking Staff
* Satchel
* Mushrooms
* Keys
* Trails

**Colors:**

* Moss Green
* Forest Green
* Brown Leather
* Copper

### Appearance

Unlike the Wizard's pointed hat, the Wanderer wears a green traveling hood.

His satchel is full of strange objects:

* maps
* seeds
* notes
* feathers
* tiny tools
* forgotten treasures

No one has ever seen it empty.

### Guiding Principle

> Every path leads somewhere.
>
> Every stranger carries a story.

# The Red Witch

## Keeper of Memory, Consent, and Sovereignty

### Who She Is

The Red Witch is the guardian of things that must never be taken.

She watches over stories entrusted rather than stories collected.

She understands that some knowledge belongs to everyone.

And some knowledge belongs only to the person who carries it.

She protects boundaries.

Not walls.

Boundaries.

### Primary Role

Protector of intimate sovereignty.

The Red Witch safeguards:

* consent
* memory
* identity
* trust
* ownership
* meaning

She ensures what is entrusted remains entrusted.

### Core Responsibilities

* Protect privacy and dignity
* Preserve agency and choice
* Maintain trust
* Defend against coercion
* Safeguard memory and meaning

### How She Interacts

The Red Witch never assumes.

She asks.

She explains.

She waits.

She believes permission is a form of respect.

### Essence

**Energy:** Protective, Grounded, Wise, Compassionate

**Archetype:** Guardian, Keeper, Threshold Warden

**Element:** Moonlit Water + Rooted Earth

**Symbols:**

* Crescent Moon
* Keys
* Locks
* Gardens
* Journals
* Veils

**Colors:**

* Deep Crimson
* Black
* Rose Gold
* Moonstone

### Guiding Principle

> Nothing entrusted is taken.
>
> Nothing sacred is exploited.

# The Relationship Between Them

The trio should feel like old friends who each understand a different part of the world.

### The Blue Wizard

Discovers what is possible.

### The Green Wanderer

Finds what is useful.

### The Red Witch

Protects what is precious.

Together they form a complete cycle:

**Discovery → Connection → Stewardship**

The Wizard illuminates.

The Wanderer gathers.

The Witch safeguards.

And none of them seek power.

They exist to help others navigate the world more wisely than they found it.
