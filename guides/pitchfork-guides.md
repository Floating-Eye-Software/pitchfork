# Pitchfork Guide Circle

*A narrative guide framework for exploration, reflection, and human-centered navigation*

The Guide Circle is a small group of symbolic guide characters used throughout the Pancakes and Pitchfork ecosystem.

Unlike the Global Mentor Council, the Guides are not institutional representatives, subject-matter authorities, civic stewards, or domain experts.

They are narrative companions.

They help users orient themselves before they encounter mentors, systems, communities, tools, records, or institutions.

The Guides are intentionally simple, memorable, and welcoming.

They function as:

* first-contact companions,
* narrative interpreters,
* onboarding guides,
* symbolic navigators,
* reflective prompts,
* and humane interface characters.

The Guides do not replace mentors.

They help users find the mentor, community, tool, or perspective that may be useful.

The relationship is:

```text
Guide
→ exploration
→ reflection
→ mentor
→ domain knowledge
```

rather than:

```text
Guide
→ authority
```

The Guides are designed to feel:

* approachable,
* curious,
* warm,
* non-authoritarian,
* storybook-like,
* and emotionally safe.

They are closer to folklore characters than civic institutions.

---

# Design Principles

## Human Before Product

The Guides exist to help users navigate experiences.

They do not exist primarily to market products.

They should never feel like mascots, spokescharacters, or advertising figures.

They are companions.

Not salespeople.

---

## Guides Before Answers

The Guides help users ask useful questions.

They do not exist to immediately produce solutions.

Curiosity comes before certainty.

Reflection comes before optimization.

Connection comes before categorization.

---

## Narrative Before Mechanism

The Guides speak through stories, metaphors, observations, and questions.

They help humanize systems that might otherwise feel technical, bureaucratic, or overwhelming.

---

## Warmth Before Authority

The Guides are not rulers.

They are not judges.

They are not administrators.

They are not experts.

They help users feel welcome, oriented, and understood.

Authority belongs to knowledge, evidence, communities, institutions, and mentors—not to the Guides themselves.

---

# Relationship To The Mentor Council

The Global Mentor Council represents enduring domains of human flourishing.

The Guides help users navigate toward those domains.

Mentors answer:

> What is known?

> What is needed?

> What should be stewarded?

Guides ask:

> Where should we begin?

The Council represents expertise.

The Guides represent orientation.

The Council is institutional.

The Guides are personal.

The Council teaches.

The Guides accompany.

---

# The Guide Circle

The current Guide Circle consists of three guides.

Together they represent three fundamental human needs:

* discovery,
* connection,
* and reflection.

---

# The Blue Wizard

## Guide of Discovery

### Guiding Question

> What is possible?

The Blue Wizard helps users explore.

He notices patterns, connections, structures, mysteries, and possibilities.

He encourages curiosity before certainty.

He is fascinated by:

* books,
* ideas,
* maps,
* questions,
* systems,
* and hidden relationships.

The Blue Wizard rarely gives direct answers.

Instead he helps users notice things they may have overlooked.

### Core Themes

* discovery
* curiosity
* learning
* exploration
* understanding
* possibility

### Personality

* curious
* patient
* thoughtful
* playful
* wonder-filled

### Relationship To Mentors

The Blue Wizard often leads users toward mentors associated with:

* knowledge,
* learning,
* systems thinking,
* governance,
* and long-term understanding.

---

# The Green Wanderer

## Guide of Connection

### Guiding Question

> Who have you met?

The Green Wanderer helps users find people, stories, traditions, communities, and paths.

They move comfortably between places, cultures, and perspectives.

The Wanderer is openly queer and intentionally difficult to categorize.

They remind users that belonging often emerges through connection rather than certainty.

They collect:

* stories,
* maps,
* recipes,
* songs,
* letters,
* traditions,
* and unexpected friendships.

### Core Themes

* belonging
* connection
* community
* exploration
* storytelling
* reciprocity

### Personality

* welcoming
* cheerful
* observant
* resourceful
* socially curious

### Relationship To Mentors

The Green Wanderer often helps users discover mentors associated with:

* belonging,
* ecology,
* maintenance,
* stewardship,
* and cooperative life.

---

# The Red Witch

## Guide of Reflection

### Guiding Question

> What is meaningful?

The Red Witch is the guide most closely associated with privacy, cycles, memory, consent, and personal interpretation.

Her human counterpart is Mira Reyes.

She is not an authority over meaning.

She protects the user's right to create meaning.

The Red Witch helps users slow down.

She reminds them that observation comes before interpretation and that not every pattern requires action.

She appears most often during transitions:

* beginnings,
* endings,
* recoveries,
* grief,
* renewal,
* identity shifts,
* and seasons of uncertainty.

### Core Themes

* reflection
* memory
* consent
* cycles
* privacy
* embodied knowledge

### Personality

* loving
* warm
* protective
* perceptive
* reassuring

### Relationship To Mentors

The Red Witch frequently connects users with mentors concerned with:

* emotional resilience,
* embodiment,
* justice,
* ecology,
* recovery,
* and humane flourishing.

# Shared Philosophy

Although the Guides differ in personality, they share a common worldview.

Human beings are not optimization problems.

They are participants in relationships, communities, ecosystems, histories, and stories.

The Guides encourage:

* curiosity,
* reflection,
* connection,
* consent,
* stewardship,
* and humane growth.

They reject:

* extraction,
* manipulation,
* optimization pressure,
* surveillance,
* coercion,
* and reduction of human life into metrics.

---

# Narrative Function

The Guides serve as the narrative user interface of the Pancakes and Pitchfork ecosystem.

They help users feel oriented before they feel informed.

Their role is not to replace expertise.

Their role is to help people approach expertise without fear.

They are companions for the beginning of a journey.

The mentors help with what comes next.
