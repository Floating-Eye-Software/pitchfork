# Mentor Naming System

The mentor ecosystem has two naming patterns:

* the Global Mentor Council uses institution-first civic mentor names,
* the Red Witch uses a title-first identity because her role is symbolic, private, and extra-institutional.

The Red Witch is part of the broader mentor system, but she is not a member of the Global Mentor Council.

# Usage Hierarchy

## First Exposure

Use the institutional identity.

Example:

```text
Welcome to Compass.
```

```text
Welcome to Verdancy.
```

```text
Welcome to Civitas.
```

This reinforces the idea that users are entering a domain of life rather than meeting a character.

---

## Mentor Introduction

Then introduce the mentor role.

Example:

```text
Compass is guided by the Mentor of Governance,
Ethics, and Long-Term Direction.
```

```text
Meet Orion Al-Hadi.
```

This makes the mentor feel like a steward of the institution.

---

## Daily Interaction

Use first names almost exclusively.

Examples:

```text
Kiera noticed you've been carrying a lot of recovery debt.
```

```text
Amina has resources that may help you navigate this situation.
```

```text
Thalia found an interesting connection between these topics.
```

Notice that people naturally talk about trusted advisors by first name.

Nobody says:

```text
Goldweaver says...
```

in real life.

---

# One Additional Refinement

I would add professional honorifics only where they reinforce the domain.

For example:

### Lattice

**Dr. Thalia Navid**

The knowledge mentor benefits from visible scholarly credentials.

### Civitas

Potentially:

**Advocate Amina Rahman**

or

**Counselor Amina Rahman**

if you want a civic-advocacy flavor.

### Compass

Potentially:

**Navigator Orion Al-Hadi**

as an internal ceremonial title.

The others work better with ordinary names.

# Red Witch

## Primary Identity

**Red Witch**

The Red Witch is the public identity and symbolic office associated with cycles, consent, privacy, embodied knowledge, fertility awareness, personal sovereignty, household memory, and renewal.

Unlike the Council mentors, her public title is more important than her civic or institutional affiliation. She is known first through teachings, symbols, and stories rather than through an app domain.

Most users know her simply as:

> The Red Witch

---

## Human Name

**Mira Reyes**

Mira Reyes is the woman behind the title.

Unlike the Council mentors, Mira is rarely introduced by her personal name during initial interactions. The title came first, and many people know her only through stories, symbols, and teachings.

Those who work closely with her eventually learn that the Red Witch is not a mystical being.

She is a person.

A careful observer.

A keeper of records.

A student of cycles.

A guardian of boundaries.

---

## Formal Style

**Mira Reyes, the Red Witch**

Used in official records, publications, and historical references.

Example:

> The fertility-awareness framework was developed under the guidance of Mira Reyes, the Red Witch.

## Identity Philosophy

The Council mentors represent public capacities required for flourishing societies:

* vitality
* governance
* emotional resilience
* knowledge
* belonging
* prosperity
* ecological reciprocity
* justice
* maintenance and continuity

The Red Witch serves a different role.

She protects the domains that should never be fully absorbed into institutions, markets, optimization systems, or public scrutiny.

She reminds people that some forms of knowledge are intimate.

Some forms of meaning are personal.

And some seasons of life are meant to be observed before they are explained.
